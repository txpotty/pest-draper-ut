Ant Infestations

Ants are a frequent nuisance, often entering homes in search of food and water. While many species are harmless, they can contaminate food and become a significant annoyance. Large ant colonies can be difficult to eliminate using DIY methods. Our professional ant control services identify the colony’s source and employ effective treatments to eradicate them completely.

Spiders are common in and around homes. While most are not dangerous, encountering venomous spiders like the Black Widow or Brown Recluse can be a serious concern. Even non-venomous spiders can create unsightly webs and cause anxiety. Our spider removal services safely eliminate spiders and help prevent them from returning.

Rodent Intrusions

Mice and rats can cause significant damage to property by gnawing on wires and structures. They also pose health risks through their droppings and urine and can contaminate food sources. Rodents reproduce quickly, making timely intervention crucial. Our rodent control strategies include trapping, exclusion, and sanitation recommendations to eliminate and prevent infestations.

Cockroach Problems

Cockroaches are notorious for being difficult to eradicate and are associated with unsanitary conditions. We provide effective cockroach control treatments that target these resilient pests.

Our bed bug extermination services are designed to effectively eliminate these elusive pests from your home.

Wasp and Bee Threats

While bees are important pollinators, wasps and some bee species can pose a stinging threat, especially to individuals with allergies. Mosquito Menace

Mosquitoes are not only annoying but can also transmit diseases like West Nile Virus. They breed in standing water and can make outdoor spaces unbearable during warmer months. Effective mosquito control involves targeting breeding sites and implementing treatments to reduce populations. We offer mosquito control solutions to help you enjoy your yard again.

Flea Infestations

Fleas are a common problem, particularly for pet owners. They cause itchy bites on both pets and humans and can quickly infest carpets and furniture. Eliminating fleas requires treating both the animals and the environment. Our flea treatment services address the entire lifecycle of fleas for effective eradication.

Silverfish are attracted to moisture and feed on paper, glue, and starches, causing damage to books, wallpaper, and other household items. They are often found in bathrooms, kitchens, and basements. While not a health threat, they can be a nuisance and cause damage. Our silverfish control services help eliminate these pantry pests.

Protecting your property from ticks is important for health and safety. We provide tick control solutions to reduce tick populations in your yard.

Moth Damage

Certain species of moths can damage clothing, carpets, and stored food products. Clothes moths feed on natural fibers, while pantry moths infest dry goods. Identifying the type of moth is essential for effective treatment. Our moth control services eliminate moth infestations and protect your belongings.

Squirrel Shenanigans

Squirrels can cause damage by gnawing on wires and entering attics and walls. While they may seem harmless, they can create fire hazards and introduce parasites. Signs You Need Professional Pest Control

Being aware of the signs of a pest infestation can help you address the problem quickly. Ignoring early signs can lead to a more severe and difficult-to-treat infestation. If you notice any of these indicators, it’s time to call in the professionals at Draper Pest Pros for pest control in Draper, UT.

Visible Pests

Seeing live pests crawling, flying, or nesting in your home or business is the most obvious sign of an infestation. Even a single sighting can indicate a larger problem, as many pests hide and reproduce quickly.

Droppings or Urine Stains

Pest droppings are a clear indicator of their presence. The size and shape of the droppings can help identify the type of pest. Urine stains and odors, particularly from rodents, also signal an infestation.

Gnaw Marks or Damage

Rodents and some insects gnaw on materials to access food or create nests. Look for gnaw marks on wood, wires, or packaging. Damaged fabrics, paper, or food items can also indicate pest activity.

Nesting Materials

Discovering nests made of shredded paper, fabric, or other materials is a strong sign of rodent or insect infestation. Nests are often found in hidden areas like attics, walls, or basements.

Unusual Odors

Some pests produce distinct odors. A musty smell can indicate rodents, while sweet or oily odors might signal cockroaches. These odors can become more pronounced as the infestation grows.

Noises in Walls or Ceilings

Scratching or scurrying sounds coming from within walls, ceilings, or attics, especially at night, are often indicative of rodent activity. Other pests like birds or squirrels can also make noise within structures.

Damage to Plants in Your Yard

Pests don’t just affect indoor spaces. Damage to your lawn, garden, or trees can indicate pest problems that require professional attention to protect your landscaping.

Webs or Egg Casings

Increased spider webs or the presence of egg casings can indicate a breeding population of spiders or other insects that the spiders are preying on.

Damage to Wiring or Plumbing

Rodents, in particular, can chew on electrical wiring or plumbing, creating fire hazards or water leaks. Any unexplained damage to these systems should be investigated.

Higher Utility Bills

In some cases, infestations can impact insulation, leading to increased energy consumption and higher utility bills. This can be a less obvious sign of a pest problem.

The Impact of Skipping Professional Pest Control

Health Risks to Occupants

Pests can carry and spread a variety of diseases and allergens. Ignoring an infestation can expose occupants to health risks, including respiratory problems, skin irritation, and more serious illnesses.

Pests reproduce quickly. A small initial problem can rapidly escalate into a full-blown infestation that is much more difficult and expensive to control.

Ineffective DIY Attempts
